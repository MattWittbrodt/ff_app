Below is a chart detailing each wide receiver versus cornerback matchup for Week 2, target rates, cornerback yards per target and reception, catch rate allowed, yards, and fantasy points per target allowed. Keep in mind, the data is only a week's worth, however, we have an idea of which secondaries to target and will also use our own knowledge of how good or bad the cornerbacks are. The two most important columns are fantasy points per target and the last one (+/-), which indicates whether the matchup is an upgrade or downgrade for the receiver (N indicates a neutral matchup).

For up-to-the-minute and Sunday morning updates, be sure to join the 4for4 DFS subscription-only Discord channel.

After the chart, I also provide key notes such as veteran receivers that will square off against rookies, how a wide receiver matchup might affect the team's tight end, etc. There are some key injuries to watch throughout the rest of the week and this chart will be updated to reflect breaking news.

Julio Jones, Falcons vs. Trevon Diggs, Cowboys

Jones received 12 targets, as did Calvin Ridley and Russell Gage, in Week 1. Jones is going to continue to receive heavy target volume, keeping Trevon Diggs very busy this week. Diggs was targeted just three times last week, however, he's going to see more action against the Falcons. Although he didn't allow a TD, he did allow 63 yards in coverage, resulting in 2.3 fantasy points per target.

T.Y. Hilton, Colts, vs. Cameron Dantzler, Vikings Mike Hughes, Vikings

The Minnesota Vikings' secondary is another group we'll be able to confidently pick on. Aaron Rodgers picked the group apart with precision. Hilton and Parris Campbell each received nine targets from Philip Rivers. All of the Colts' receivers are in play this week, but Hilton has double-TD upside in his matchup with Hughes. Cameron Dantzler was expected to get the nod, but he's been ruled out for the week. Hilton will move around, so he'll also run routes on Holton Hill. Hughes was targeted six times last week, allowing a 100% catch rate. He was also responsible for a score, yielding a fantasy-friendly 6.9 points per target. Hill was targeted 11 times and allowed 2.3 fantasy points per target.

Mike Evans, Buccaneers vs. Donte Jackson, Panthers

The Panthers are another secondary that's in a bad way. Donte Jackson has practiced, but his status is still in question. Chris Godwin has entered concussion protocol and his availability is in doubt—it doesn't bode well that he entered protocol Wednesday. This is shaping up to be a get-right game for Mike Evans and the Tampa offense. He's still being limited in practice, however, it's likely out of precaution. He was just about doubtful for Week 1 and ended up playing in 93% of the snaps in a tough matchup. He gets a favorable matchup against a soft Carolina secondary. If he avoids Jackson, he'll draw Troy Pride, who got burned for 106 yards and a score on six targets.

Michael Gallup, Cowboys vs. Isaiah Oliver, Falcons

Gallup was an arm extension from recording over 90 yards Sunday night. The Atlanta Falcons appear to be another secondary we can exploit. Falcons' corners were targeted by Russell Wilson 20 times, allowing 17 catches. Oliver allowed six of his eight targets to be caught, allowing a TD and 2.5 fantasy points per target.

Keenan Allen, Chargers vs. Antonio Hamilton, Chiefs

While the sky is falling for Austin Ekeler, the song remains the same for Keenan Allen with eight targets last week. Allen should run most of his routes from the slot, leaving him on Hamilton. Hamilton allowed four of his six targets to be caught, resulting in 2.5 fantasy points per target. I'd love to see Shane Steichen scheme him to run routes on Ben Niemann. Niemann allowed 56 yards and a score in coverage.
