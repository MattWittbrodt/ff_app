Below is a chart detailing each wide receiver versus cornerback matchup for Week 3, target rates, cornerback yards per target and reception, catch rate allowed, yards, and fantasy points per target allowed. Keep in mind, the data is only a week's worth, however, we have an idea of which secondaries to target and will also use our own knowledge of how good or bad the cornerbacks are. The two most important columns are fantasy points per target and the last one (+/-), which indicates whether the matchup is an upgrade or downgrade for the receiver (N indicates a neutral matchup).

For up-to-the-minute and Sunday morning updates, be sure to join the 4for4 DFS subscription-only Discord channel.

After the chart, I also provide key notes such as veteran receivers that will square off against rookies, how a wide receiver matchup might affect the team's tight end, etc. There are some key injuries to watch throughout the rest of the week and this chart will be updated to reflect breaking news.

Steve Sims, Washington Football Team vs. Tavierre Thomas, Browns

I hope the Football Teamers watched the film from last week's Bengals game. They'd see that Tavierre Thomas allowed nine of his 10 targets to be caught. Sims saw a slight increase in targets from Week 1 to Week 2, so I'm hoping that trend continues into Sunday. Thomas is allowing 10.3 yards per reception and 2.5 fantasy points per target.

Tyler Boyd, Bengals vs. Nickell Robey-Coleman, Eagles

Over the first few weeks of the season, NRC has been thrown at six times, however, I see that number increasing significantly this week. A.J. Green has commanded most of Joe Burrow's attention, Green will draw Darius Slay's shadow and as a result, Boyd's matchup will allow him to have a better week. It's a small sample in two games, however, NRC has allowed a 100% catch rate, yielding 3.3 fantasy points per target.

Will Fuller, Texans vs. Steven Nelson, Steelers

Joe Haden doesn't shadow and spends nearly 100% of his coverage routes on the left side, so he should matchup on Brandin Cooks for most of the day. Fuller does move around, however, and should spend about 40%-45% on the left side and run his routes on Steven Nelson, who's been below average in coverage this season. I'm hoping the Texans keep him out of the slot. Nelson has allowed a touchdown in each of the first two games, resulting in 2.5 fantasy points per target average. I don't foresee Fuller not being targeted again this week.

Mike Williams, Chargers vs. Donte Jackson, Panthers

No mention of air yards here, just Donte Jackson is not a good cover corner and won't match up well with Wiliams this week. Thankfully, Justin Herbert is starting once again. Jackson had some troubles with Mike Evans last week and Williams is similar in stature. Williams can get the jump balls and is a red zone threat. Williams is the second-most targeted receiver, however, he did see a decrease in targets from Week 1 to Week 2, something to keep in the back of your mind. I'm hoping Herbert takes advantage of the matchup here. Williams has an aDOT (average depth of target) of 16.2, while Jackson is allowing 14.0 yards per target. Over Jackson's last 63 times he's been thrown at, he's has allowed a 65% completion rate and 2.4 fantasy points per target.

Damiere Byrd, Patriots vs. Damon Arnette, Raiders

N'Keal Harry and Julian Edelman are Cam Newton's main targets, but Byrd saw nine targets last week and has a prime match-up opposite rookie CB, Damon Arnette. Byrd saw nine targets last week, converting six into catches and averaged 12 yards per catch. If the Pats get near the red zone, Newton, more than likely, is going to take it himself. Byrd is a deep threat and Arnette is giving up 19.4 yards per target. In his short career, Arnette is allowing 3.6 fantasy points per target.

Tyler Lockett, Seahawks vs. Jourdan Lewis, Cowboys

I like both of the 'Hawks' pass catchers this week—DK Metcalf is going to abuse Diggs. Tyler Lockett also has a favorable matchup in the slot opposite Jourdan Lewis. Dating back to last season, Lewis has been targeted 62 times while allowing a 71% catch rate. He was back in the lineup in Week 2 and proceeded to allow four of five targets to be caught, one for a score. Over the last 62 times he's been thrown at, he's allowing 2.01 fantasy points per target.
