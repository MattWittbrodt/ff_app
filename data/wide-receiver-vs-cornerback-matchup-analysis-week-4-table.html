Below is a chart detailing each wide receiver versus cornerback matchup for Week 4, target rates, cornerback yards per target and reception, catch rate allowed, yards, and fantasy points per target allowed. We're a few weeks in and starting to get an idea of which corners to target. We also know have which secondaries to target and will also use our own knowledge of how good or bad the cornerbacks are. The two most important columns are fantasy points per target and the last one (+/-), which indicates whether the matchup is an upgrade or downgrade for the receiver (N indicates a neutral matchup).

For up-to-the-minute and Sunday morning updates, be sure to join the 4for4 DFS subscription-only Discord channel.

After the chart, I also provide key notes such as veteran receivers that will square off against rookies, how a wide receiver matchup might affect the team's tight end, etc. There are some key injuries to watch throughout the rest of the week and this chart will be updated to reflect breaking news.

Jarvis Landry, Browns vs. Jourdan Lewis, Cowboys

Welcome to slot week here at 4for4. First up, we have Jarvis Landry matching up with Jourdan Lewis. The Seahawks aired it out last week and targeted Lewis just three times in Week 3. Landry's targets have been all over the place this season—after seeing six in Week 1, he's received just seven over the last two games. Kareem Hunt has siphoned targets away, as he has a higher target/snap rate this season. I think Baker tosses out some quick hitches and check-downs to neutralize Aldon Smith in an effort to get the ball out quick. Lewis allowed a TD in Week 1 and Landry can squeeze through the second level.

Odell Beckham, Browns vs. Trevon Diggs, Cowboys

I also like Beckham in the same matchup. It appears the Browns' offensive line has improved and limited pressure from Washington last week—granted Chase Young left early, but they are stout up front. If Baker gets time, he could hit Beckham for some big plays against Trevon Diggs. Diggs is allowing 10.6 yards per target and 16.3 yards per reception, resulting in two fantasy points per target.

Tyler Boyd, Bengals vs. DJ Hayden, Jaguars

Tyler Boyd seems to be Joe Burrow's schmoopy. If you saw the Soup Nazi episode, Jerry's and his girlfriend were not afraid of PDAs and called each other schmoopy—however, he did choose soup over her. I don't see Burrow choosing a medium crab bisque over one of his favorite receivers. Boyd has 21 targets over his last two games, converting 17 into catches. The Jaguars, as a defense, have allowed six scores to the opposing slot receivers. Hayden had an easier go in Week 3, but in his previous two games, he had allowed 151 yards and a score. On the year, D.J. Hayden is allowing nearly three fantasy points per target.

Will Fuller, Texans vs. Holton Hill, Vikings

As you can see from the chart, you can play any of the Texans' receivers this week. Houston's implied total is 28.75, so I'm making the assumption that Fuller is going to heavily involved in the gameplan. Fuller, after receiving no targets in Week 2, saw five and hit paydirt with K-Dirt—hitting you with a Ford Fairlane reference there—with a TD. Hill has been targeted by opposing QBs on 17% of their throws and is allowing 15.8 yards per catch and 2.2 fantasy points per target.

DeVante Parker, Dolphins vs. Shaquill Griffen, Seahawks

Even though Griffin was able to record an INT, he still had a rough day at the office last week. Griffen was picked on to the tune of 12 targets and allowed 151 yards. Parker has 13 targets over the last two weeks and should match up on Griggin for most of his routes this week. Griffin is the second-most targeted CB and is allowing nearly 2.5 fantasy points per target.

Isaiah Ford, Dolphins vs. Slot Corners, Seahawks

Staying in this game, Ford is shaping up to be a value playing against Seattle's defense and their weakness against slot receivers. No defense has allowed more yards to opposing slot receivers than the Seahawks (77). Ford should be matched up on Ugo Amandi, but if they watch the film, the Dolphins can get him lined up on K.J. Wright and Shaquem Griffin. Overall, the Seahawks defense when covering the slot, they're allowing 2.2 fantasy points per target.

Marquise Brown, Ravens vs. Ronald Darby, Washington Football Team

It's possible we see angry Ravens Sunday after their Monday performance against the Chiefs. Brown's targets have been consistent this season—averaging six per game—but he's just missed out on some big plays with Lamar Jackson, including two against KC. Brown has an average depth of target of 13.5 while Ronald Darby is yielding 13.4 yards per target. If Brown can get loose, Darby is allowing 61.5% of balls thrown his way to be caught, he could be in a big day. Even though he has to allow a TD, Darby is allowing nearly two fantasy points per target.
