Wide Receiver vs. Cornerback Matchup Analysis: Divisional Round

Mike Woellert
Author twitter link

Getty Images

When it comes to selecting receivers in DFS, it’s important to understand who will cover them. Many times defense-versus-position stats alone don't tell the whole story or overlooked defensive injuries will have a significant effect.

Below is a chart detailing each wide receiver versus cornerback matchup for the Divisional Round, target rates, cornerback yards per target and reception, catch rate allowed and yards per target allowed. Keep in mind, it's the playoffs, so the defenses are better, however, that doesn't mean there isn't a matchup or two we can exploit. The two most important columns are fantasy points per target and the last one (+/-), which indicates whether the matchup is an upgrade or downgrade for the receiver (N indicates a neutral matchup).

After the chart, I also provide key notes such as veteran receivers that will square off against rookies, how a wide receiver matchup might affect the team's tight end, etc. There are some key injuries to watch throughout the rest of the week and this chart will be updated to reflect breaking news.

Deebo Samuel, 49ers vs. Xavier Rhodes, Vikings

The Vikings secondary is beat up, including Xavier Rhodes is dealing with an ankle and shoulder injury. Although Rhodes was targeted four times last week, he allowed 70 yards in coverage. I don’t see Jimmy Garoppolo shying away from one of his favorite targets. If San Francisco is going to put up nearly 26 points, you have to assume Samuel is involved. Rhodes was targeted on nearly 15% of opposing QBs throws, allowing a 76.5% catch rate.

Mark Andrews, Ravens vs. Logan Ryan, Titans

Andrews, during the season, ran 64% of his routes from the slot. He should see Ryan on the majority of his routes, which is a plus-matchup for Andrews. Surprisingly, Ryan saw just three targets last week. I think that increases this week as Lamar Jackson should frequently throw toward his favorite target. Andrews should also see some of Kevin Byard’s coverage. Combined, Ryan and Byard allowed nine scores during the season. They’re going to have a tough time covering Andrews.

Stefon Diggs, Vikings vs. Ahkello Witherspoon, 49ers

Diggs is missing practice due to an illness, but Mike Zimmer expressed confidence in his receiver’s availability this weekend. Diggs should see Witherspoon on his most of his routes with Thielen on the other side working on Richard Sherman. There’s the possibility Diggs sees some Sherman coverage. Witherspoon has struggled the most among 49er cornerbacks. He’s been responsible for seven scores and is allowing a robust 2.2 fantasy points per target.

Tyreek Hill, Chiefs vs. Gareon Conley, Texans

Conley has had a bit of a resurgence in Houston, but was targeted heavily by Josh Allen in the Wild Card Round—15 times. A lot of Allen's passes were off the mark, but Conley still allowed 68 yards in coverage. He should see a lot of Tyreek Hill this week and Patrick Mahomes is more accurate than Allen. If Hill burns Conley, even just once, he could take a Mahomes pass to the house in the blink of an eye.

Sammy Watkins, Chiefs vs. Vernon Hargreaves, Texans

Watkins is a lower-price receiver that allows you to fit in some of the higher-priced backs and TEs. Watkins should run most of his routes out of the slot, so he should see Hargreaves on most of his routes. Hargreaves saw eight targets last week, allowing seven completions. His regular-season struggles have bled into the playoffs, so he's going to have some problems with this offense. Over the course of the season, Hargreaves allowed seven scores and yielded 2.1 fantasy points per target.
