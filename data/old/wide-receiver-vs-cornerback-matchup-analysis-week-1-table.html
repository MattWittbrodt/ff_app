Thankfully, we're ready to kick off the 2020 season, albeit, in the middle of a global pandemic. The NFL has taken the precautions and the players followed protocols and safety procedures, so we're fortunate to have an opening week. I'm back covering the wide receivers and their cornerback matchups for 4for4.

Below is a chart detailing each wide receiver versus cornerback matchup for Week 1, target rates, cornerback yards per target and reception, catch rate allowed, yards and fantasy points per target allowed. We'll be using data from 2019 (minimum 20 targets in coverage), as well as our own knowledge of how good or bad the cornerbacks actually for the first few weeks of the season. The two most important columns are fantasy points per target and the last one (+/-), which indicates whether the matchup is an upgrade or downgrade for the receiver (N indicates a neutral matchup).

For up-to-the-minute and Sunday morning updates, be sure to join the 4for4 DFS subscription-only Discord channel.

After the chart, I also provide key notes such as veteran receivers that will square off against rookies, how a wide receiver matchup might affect the team's tight end, etc. There are some key injuries to watch throughout the rest of the week and this chart will be updated to reflect breaking news.

Allen Robinson, Bears (FD $7,200/DK $6,500) vs. Jeff Okudah, Lions

Mitchell Trubisky was named the Week 1 starter, so I'm hoping the chemistry he's built with Allen Robinson will continue this season. Robinson was targeted 153 times last season, a 9.56 per-game average. The Lions secondary is going to miss Darius Slay, and it appears Robinson will find himself lined up on rookie, Jeff Okudah—talk about being thrown to the wolves in Week 1. Robinson ran 50% of his routes in 2019 on the left side, so he should see the rookie on most of his routes. If he does move around, he'll match up with Desmond Trufant. Trufant has battled various foot injuries that have zapped some of his speed. Trufant is coming off a 2019 season in which he was responsible for four scores and yielded 2.4 fantasy points per target. No matter where Robinson lines up, he'll have a favorable matchup.

D.J. Moore, Panthers (FD $7,100/DK $6,600) vs. Damon Arnette, Raiders

We're taking advantage of another rookie corner in this matchup. Moore played most of the 2019 season with Kyle Allen at the helm and was still targeted 135 times. In 2020, he gets an upgrade at QB and a favorable matchup opposite rookie CB, Damon Arnette. Arnette is dealing with a fractured thumb, so he could be playing in a cast, which puts him at an even further disadvantage. Even if the Raiders slide Trayvon Mullen over, I still like Moore's upside this week.

DeSean Jackson, Eagles (FD $5,700/DK $4,900) vs. Fabian Moreau, Washington Football Team

You might as well take advantage of Mr. Week 1. Over his last two opening weeks, Jackson has received 14 targets, converting 13 to receptions for 300 yards and four touchdowns. Jackson appears fully healthy and Philadelphia will be without Alshon Jeffrey and Jalen Reagor. Jackson and Zach Ertz should be the primary pass catchers with some John Hightower and Greg Ward mixed in. I like Jackson's matchup on Fabian Moreau the most. Moreau allowed 73% of his targets to be caught while yielding 2.1 fantasy points per target.

Stefon Diggs, Bills ($6,600/DK $6,400) vs. Pierre Desir, Jets

John Brown will likely draw more ownership in this one, but I really like Stefon Diggs's favorable matchup opposite Pierre Desir this week. Diggs produced an aDOT (average depth of target) of 14.8 and Desir yielded 9.8 yards per target, so I'm not sure he's going to be able to run well with Diggs this week. Desir allowed 16.3 yards per reception and just under two fantasy points per target.

D.J. Chark, Jaguars (FD $6,600/DK $7,700) vs. Xavier Rhodes, Colts

Targeting Rhodes last season typically produced good results. Chark enjoyed a breakout year with Gardner Minshew at the helm, receiving 117 targets, including 20 targets in two games against Indy last season. Rhodes had a rough 2019 season, allowing 77% of his targets to be caught and four scores while failing to produce an INT. He yielded 2.2 fantasy points per target. Even if Chark moves around, he'll find himself on Rock Ya-Sin, who has a great name, but allowed just under two fantasy points per game at 1.98. Chark is a nice spot to produce. Especially with Leonard Fournette out of the picture, there might be more emphasis on the passing game.

Kenny Golladay, Lions (FD $7,300/DK $6,200) vs. Jaylon Johnson, Bears

Golladay and Marvin Jones could be playing a weekly game of ping pong to decide whose turn it is to have the big week. In Week 1, it's Golladay. Golladay should run the majority of his routes on rookie corner, Jaylon Johnson. In 2019, Golladay ran 51% from the left side, so that should have him matched up on Johnson for most of the game. Golladay ran 33% of his routes on the right, which would match him up on Kyle Fuller, who yielded 1.78 points per target last season.

Update 09/10/2020: Kenny Golladay was at practice, but not doing any individual drills. Keep an eye on his status throughout the rest of the week into Sunday. Marvin Jones would soak up Golladay's targets in a favorable matchup.
